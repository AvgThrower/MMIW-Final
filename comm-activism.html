Indigenous Led Movement : The REDress Project

The Redress Project was an installation of red dresses made to focus on the MMIW crisis created by Jaime Black, a member of the Metis nation. Her goal was to bring awareness to racialized and gendered crimes against Indigenous women and people. The project places the Indigenous women's body as a target of violence. Through doing this she reclaimed space for Indigenous women's presence. She attempted to create a conversation about the social and political issues involving Indigenous people

MMIW Advocacy : What you can do

  • May 5th is Missing & Murdered Indigenous Persons Awareness Day also known as Red Dress Day. On this day it's recommended you wear red, use your social media platform, and more to bring awareness and fight the injustices Indigenous people face

  • Educate yourself. You can do research of your own on people's stories and experiences to understand the impact this crisis has on Indigenous communities
